# Story Bible

This file is the canonical memory for narrative direction. It separates accepted premise, implemented story state, planned arcs, and deliberately open questions so future content does not accidentally present an idea as finished lore.

## Accepted Premise

**Battle of Gods** begins as an isekai story. Opaw was killed in a former world and awakened beyond death in the **Sanctuary of the Remembered Veil**. His previous life's final moments are fractured; the killer, motive, and exact death remain mysteries that may be revealed gradually rather than explained in an opening exposition dump.

Most known gods do not value mortal life as mortals do. Worlds, wars, chosen heroes, and catastrophes can become trials, wagers, or entertainment to them. This makes divine power dangerous even when it appears beautiful. Not every god must be identical: some may be cruel, indifferent, trapped by divine law, secretly sympathetic, or openly opposed to the games.

**The One Above** predates and exceeds the known gods. It remains the strongest known being and a mystery rather than an ordinary quest-giver or the automatically confirmed final villain. The lesser gods' cruelty does not yet prove The One Above created or approves their games.

## Opaw and the Veil

Opaw is the first playable soul and begins as a weak **Novice Warrior**, not a chosen demigod. His early Ashwood Blade and grounded Sweeping Cut make later supernatural growth meaningful.

The Remembered Veil preserves impressions that death and divine interference could not fully erase. Sanctuary is a refuge where displaced souls can recover identity, learn which memories are truly theirs, and enter hostile realms through earned expedition paths. Eira studies soul-memory and sealed power. Orren prepares mortal weapons because honest craft remains valuable even against divine enemies.

The first story question is not “How powerful can Opaw become?” but “Why did the Veil remember him when death should have ended his story?”

## Playable Characters and Classes

The planned game supports a **switchable roster of playable souls** gathered from different worlds or eras. Character identity and combat class are separate concepts:

- A character owns a name, history, visual identity, voice, relationships, and personal memory progression.
- A class owns a combat vocabulary such as Warrior, Mage, or Archer, including compatible weapons, movement, and skill families.
- Opaw is the first character and currently uses the Warrior foundation.
- Future Mage and Archer development may begin with distinct characters or later class paths, but this choice is not implemented yet.
- Character switching should occur in Sanctuary or another safe preparation space, never as an unexplained mid-attack body replacement.

This boundary permits future original guest-like characters without rewriting the player controller. Any publishable crossover character must be original or properly licensed; inspiration alone does not make another creator's character part of Battle of Gods canon.

## Power Escalation

Escalation should feel enormous while preserving readable telegraphs, dodge mastery, and meaningful punish windows.

1. **Mortal danger:** corrupted creatures, thralls, warriors, and local tyrants.
2. **Chosen danger:** champions, divine beasts, angels, demigods, and reality-touched bosses.
3. **God danger:** named gods whose arenas and phase rules express their domain.
4. **Beyond-god danger:** beings outside the known pantheon that treat gods as limited creatures.
5. **Boundless danger:** entities that distort distance, causality, death, or world law.
6. **Author-class danger:** a possible far-future meta-cosmic tier able to rewrite the rules of a realm itself. The name, nature, and place of this tier remain provisional.

Huge swords, catastrophic spells, impossible movement, and extreme damage belong in later bands, but spectacle must never replace player agency. A god-tier attack is exciting because the player understands and survives it, not because the screen becomes unreadable.

## Opening Story Path

### Prologue — The Soul the Veil Remembered

Opaw awakens in Sanctuary with no clear explanation for why he crossed worlds. The portal opens only after Sanctuary records that awakening. Eira recognizes that parts of his soul are sealed rather than missing.

### Chapter I — Forgotten Grove

The Grove is the first available expedition. Its corruption has begun to imitate memory, suggesting that the monsters are consequences of deliberate divine interference rather than random decay. The currently playable two-stage route establishes this mystery and records completion in story memory.

### Implemented Mini-Boss Expedition — The Rootbound Hollow

A corrupted hollow reveals that the small Rootlings behave as the Rootbound Husk's brood. After Opaw cuts through ten of them, the ancient guardian names the deaths as a trespass before confronting him as the first mini-boss. Its authored normal-access contract currently requires level 3, completion of the Forgotten Grove story memory, victory over the planned **Thornbound Warden**, and the planned **Cinder Sigil** key item. The playable scene is available through F9 for development, while normal access remains sealed until those requirements can be earned.

### Planned Continuation — The Drowned Bells

A drowned sanctuary still calls to gods that abandoned it. Its authored access contract includes higher level, story, boss, discovery, and key-item memories. The route and its content remain planned.

## Story Delivery

- Prefer short dialogue, locations, enemy design, artifacts, boss phases, and recovered memories over long exposition.
- Let Eira interpret memory without making her automatically correct about every mystery.
- Let Orren ground the story in mortal survival, materials, and the cost of divine conflict.
- Record completed facts as stable story IDs; keep display text and localization separate from those IDs.
- A story flag means an event occurred. It must not silently grant equipment, combat stats, or class ownership.

## Open Questions

- Who or what caused Opaw's death in his former world?
- Did the Veil choose Opaw, or did someone place his soul there?
- Which god first turned mortal struggle into entertainment?
- Is The One Above absent, observing, imprisoned, or operating under rules unknown even to gods?
- Are Warrior, Mage, and Archer fixed character identities, selectable class paths, or both through different characters?
- What unlocks the first playable character switch, and what progression belongs to the roster versus one character?
- Which early boss closes the full Forgotten Grove arc, and where are the Cinder Sigil and Thornbound Warden introduced?
